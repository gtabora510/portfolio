# Content Style Guide

Access this style guide to ensure consistency in the content across Developer Portal.

## About the Developer Portal Content Style Guide

The Developer Portal content style guide outlines the guidelines to be followed to establish and protect the written style and to ensure consistency in its use.

This content is continuously iterated to ensure it supports the developer portal communications strategy, priorities, and deliverables.

This guidance covers how developer portal content should be written (portal user interface content, notices, procedures, documentation, and so on.)

Guidance is provided on recommended usage as well as usage that should be avoided.

The writing guidance provided in this document is for internal communications, products, and events only. If your team has public-facing events that require official communications, follow the external communications guidelines.

## Content Suggestions/Questions

If you have suggestions or questions about the contents of this document, send them to developer.portal@acme.com.

## Developer Portal Persona

The Developer Portal is for the company's engineers. Therefore, the foundational principle of the portal is to create content from an engineer's perspective.

- Think about how an engineer would typically interact with the portal and your product, including onboarding or provisioning for the first time.
- Consider how an engineer might use your documentation or the information you are providing to solve specific issues or achieve certain objectives.

## Voice and Tone

Developer portal content should be written in a **succinctly conversational** tone and voice. This means that developer portal content should be informative and direct without being overly conversational. Engineers are accessing this content to find out how to do something. You want them to find what they need and be able to implement what they learn without extraneous words.

A succinctly conversational tone means that you should avoid being too friendly in your writing; do not use words, such as _it's_, _easy_, _simply_, or _let's_. Conversely, try not to be stilted or overly dry in your writing. Write your content in short, concise sentences. Aim to keep your sentences to less than 25 words, if possible.

Imagine an engineer is sitting next to you, and you are clearly and directly guiding them through the steps to complete a task. The following examples illustrate differences in tone.

| Avoid                                                                                                                                   | Recommended                                                                                              |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _Oops! Something's wrong with the update process. Let's see what went wrong and how you can fix it._<br>**This is too conversational.** | _Update Process Error: Discover what went wrong and how to correct it._<br>**This is the correct tone.** |
| _Error detected in update process. Learn about the error detected and its remediation steps._<br>**This is too formal.**                |                                                                                                          |

## Global Audience

It is important that you keep your writing simple and consistent for a global audience. The firm's engineers are from many different countries and cultures and English may not be their first language.

To ensure that your content is universally understood:

| Avoid                                                                               | Recommended                                                                                                  |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Do not use idioms or metaphors, as they may not be widely understood.               | Write in short sentences.                                                                                    |
| Avoid culturally specific references, such as specific holidays or sporting events. | Use active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is the person or thing performing the action. |
| Avoid references to seasons.                                                        | Write in second person. Address the engineer reading your content as _you_ or write with _you_ implied.      |
| Avoid using contractions as they may impact readability.                            |                                                                                                              |

Per the ACME Editorial Style Guide, developer portal content should be written in U.S. English using American spellings. Reference the Spelling section for more information on American spelling.

## Text Formatting and Organization

### Content Blocks

**Avoid large blocks of content**; instead, break your text into subsections to improve readability.

### Headings

**Use headings to divide your content** into topic-specific sections for readability. Headings should progress from level to level without skipping levels.

**Use sentence case with your headings.** This means that the first word is capitalized, and all other words that follow are in lowercase, except for proper nouns.

| Avoid                              | Recommended                        |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| _Text Formatting and Organization_ | _Text formatting and organization_ |

**Avoid phrasing your section headings as questions.** It is good to consider what question you may be answering in a particular section, but you do not need to title it as such.

| Avoid                | Recommended  |
| -------------------- | ------------ |
| _What do I do next?_ | _Next steps_ |

### Lists

When the order of events in a list is important, **use a numbered list.**

Recommended:

1. _Open the building's door._
2. _Walk into the lobby._

When you are listing items, but the order is irrelevant, **use a bulleted list.**

Recommended:

_You can learn more about the following languages on the Developer Portal:_

- _C#_
- _Go_
- _Java_

Reference the Capitalization section for information on capitalization in bulleted lists.

## Language and Grammar

### Abbreviations and Acronyms

**Define, or spell out, acronyms at their first instance of use,** include the acronym in parentheses () after it is spelled out.

Recommended: _You can learn more about the Developer Portal (DP) line of business at acme.com/dp. Within DP, there are four product lines._

**Use sentence style case**; this is the preferred capitalization style at the firm per the ACME Editorial Style Guide. In sentence case, the first letter of the first word is capitalized, and all other letters and words that follow are in lowercase, except for proper nouns. You should use sentence case when writing headings, field labels, button text, and so on.

**Capitalize the first letter of each item in a bulleted list.** See the Punctuation section for guidance on using periods in bulleted lists.

### Dates

**When writing dates, follow the U.S. English style** of month-day-year. Use figures for the days of the month when they follow the name of the month.

**Write the name of the month using its 3-letter abbreviation.** Do not place periods at the end of these abbreviations unless the abbreviation is the last word in the sentence.

- Jan
- Feb
- Mar
- Apr
- May
- Jun
- Jul
- Aug
- Sep
- Oct
- Nov
- Dec

When writing a day after a month, do not add _"st, nd, rd or th"_ after the number. Those suffixes are added when the date is spoken, but are incorrect in the written format.

| Avoid                                                                                                     | Recommended                                                                                                                                                |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _January 31st, 2023, is a major delivery milestone._<br>OR<br>_The target delivery date is January 31st._ | _Jan 31, 2023, is a major delivery milestone._<br>OR<br>_The next major delivery milestone is in Jan 2023._<br>OR<br>_The target delivery date is Jan 31._ |

When referencing days of the week, **write out the full name of the day**. If space is limited, use the day's 3-letter abbreviation. Do not place periods at the end of these abbreviations unless the abbreviation is the last word in the sentence.

- Mon
- Tue
- Wed
- Thu
- Fri
- Sat
- Sun

### Present Tense

**Write in present tense**; avoiding future tense.

| Avoid                                      | Recommended                            |
| ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| _The system will display your local time._ | _The system displays your local time._ |

Another component to using present tense and not future is Timeless content.

### Second Person

**Use second person.** Address the engineer reading your content as you or write with you implied. Avoid using first person language, such as _we will_, _let's_, or _our_.

| Avoid                                                | Recommended                                                     |

| _Let's review our technical writing best practices._ | _Technical writing best practices are covered in this section._ |

### Time

**Write the full name of each time unit,** whenever possible.

If space is constrained and abbreviations must be used, use the following abbreviations for units of time.

| Unit        | Abbreviation |
| ----------- | ------------ |
| Millisecond | ms           |
| Second      | s            |
| Minute      | min          |
| Hour        | hr           |

### Absolute or Relative Time

Whether you use **absolute** or **relative** timestamps depends on the context. To represent the exact date and time that an event occurred, use an absolute timestamp.

To represent how long ago an event occurred, use a relative timestamp. When reporting relative time, follow these examples:

| Time frame          | Usage examples                |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| 0-60 seconds        | Just now                      |
| 1-60 minutes        | 4 minutes ago; 16 minutes ago |
| 1-24 hours          | 4 hours ago; 18 hours ago     |
| Yesterday           | 1 day ago                     |
| 1-30 days ago       | 3 days ago; 24 days ago       |
| 1 month-1 year ago  | 4 months ago; 11 months ago   |
| 1 year+             | Apr 29, 2022                  |
| Exact date and time | Apr 29, 2022, 23:33 UTC       |

### Time of Day and Time Zones

When writing a time of day, use numerals for the time values. Times should be written on a 12-hour clock, including either AM or PM as appropriate.

| Avoid                                                                                                 | Recommended                                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| _The report is updated daily at eight in the morning._<br>OR<br>_Submit your changes by 14:00 hours._ | _The report is updated daily at 8:00 AM._<br>OR<br>_Submit your changes by 2:00 PM._ |

For the most part, time should be displayed in the user's local time on the developer portal. However, when it is necessary to express a time zone, such as for an event, use ET, PT, CT, etc. It is not necessary to include the distinction of daylight saving time or standard time.

| Avoid                                           | Recommended                                    |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| _The onboarding session starts at 8:00 AM EST._ | _The onboarding session starts at 8:00 AM ET._ |

### Timeless Content

Timeless content refers to documentation that does not contain references to time-based words, such as _currently, soon, now, new, at this time, latest,_ and so on.

Avoiding these words in documentation is important to ensuring its relevance and reducing the effort required to maintain it.

Time-based words may be used when writing time-specific content, such as release notes or incident communications.

### Units of Measure

**Be consistent when using units** and avoid mixing them.

| Avoid                                                  | Recommended                                  |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| _The data refreshes every 10 seconds to 1.25 minutes._ | _The data refreshes every 10 to 75 seconds._ |

**Use a space between numbers and units** except when you are writing about percentages.

| Avoid                                               | Recommended                                         |
| --------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| _A reduction of 25MB results in a savings of 30 %._ | _A reduction of 25 MB results in a savings of 30%._ |

**Display the full name of the unit,** unless space is constrained.

| Avoid                                           | Recommended                                            |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| _The process should complete in less than 10s._ | _The process should complete in less than 10 seconds._ |

If you must abbreviate units, consider the following:

- **Do not make unit abbreviations plural.**

| Avoid                                                      | Recommended                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The report processed and was available in about 15 mins._ | _The report processed and was available in about 15 minutes._ |

- **Do not put a period after a unit abbreviation** unless it is at the end of a sentence.

| Avoid                                                                   | Recommended                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _Reports process every 15 min. and will be available on the dashboard._ | _Reports process every 15 min. They are available on the dashboard._ |

### Word Preferences

Refer to the Terminology section under User Interface for additional guidance on preferred wording.

**Do not use _once_ to mean order of events**; use after instead. Once means one time.

| Avoid                                                      | Recommended                                          |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| _Once you enter the name of the product, select **Save.**_ | _After you enter the product name, select **Save.**_ |

**Do not use _utilize_ when you mean _use_.** Utilize refers to using something beyond its intended purpose.

| Avoid                                                                        | Recommended                                                                                            |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| _I utilize my umbrella to stay dry in the rain and as a weapon when needed._ | _Use your umbrella to stay dry in the rain. Utilize your umbrella to ward off an attacker, if needed._ |

**Use the word _engineer_ rather than the word _developer_.** As the content you are writing is for the firm's engineering staff, you should reference the role as such.

| Avoid                                                                                                                                                    | Recommended                                                                                                                                             |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The developer portal provides solutions to developers, leveraging a suite of capabilities geared toward self-service, automation, and personalization._ | _The developer portal provides solutions to engineers, leveraging a suite of capabilities geared toward self-service, automation, and personalization._ |

**Do not use an ampersand (&) in place of the word _and_.** They are not interchangeable. An ampersand should only be used in writing when it is part of an official name.

| Avoid                                                                                               | Recommended                                                                                              |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The goal is to discover & connect across a complex ecosystem of products, components & solutions._ | _The goal is to discover and connect across a complex ecosystem of products, components, and solutions._ |

**Do not use _we, our, or us._** Instead, use the name of the entity.

| Avoid                                                                                                       | Recommended                                                                                                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _Our goal is to discover and connect us across a complex ecosystem of products, components, and solutions._ | _The developer portal's goal is for the firm's engineers to discover and connect across a complex ecosystem of products, components, and solutions._ |

**Use the word _user_ to refer to an individual using the software/service** that you are developing. Otherwise, refer to the reader as _you_ or write with _you_ implied.

| Avoid                                                                                                                            | Recommended                                                                                              |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The customer should select **Save** to save their changes._<br>OR<br>_With this function, the customer can save their changes._ | _Select **Save** to save your changes._<br>OR<br>_This function enables the user to save their changes._ |

**Avoid using _text box_,** instead use _box_ or _field_ when directing your user to enter information.

| Avoid                                       | Recommended                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _Enter your name in the **Name** text box._ | _Enter your name in the **Name** field._<br>OR<br>_Enter your name in the **Name** box._ |

**Avoid using _surface_ as a verb;** instead, use a more direct term such as _make aware, expose,_ or _inform._

| Avoid                                                | Recommended                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The analytics provided surface the user's journey._ | _The analytics provided inform you of the user's journey._ |

**Avoid using _spin up_;** use a more specific term such as _create_ or _start._

| Avoid                                                              | Recommended                                                       |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The team should spin up a work stream to address the initiative._ | _The team should create a work stream to address the initiative._ |

**Avoid using _above_ or _below_ to reference other content.** Content may be reorganized, reflowed, or read out of order (for example, in search results or exported formats), so positional references can be inaccurate or confusing. Reference the specific section name instead.

| Avoid                                                         | Recommended                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| _As described above, you must configure your proxy settings._ | _As described in the Proxy Configuration section, you must configure your proxy settings._ |
| _Follow the steps below to personalize the portal._           | _Follow these steps to personalize the portal._                                            |

**Do not use _please_ when directing the reader.** State the action directly.

| Avoid                                           | Recommended                              |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| _Please enter your name in the **Name** field._ | _Enter your name in the **Name** field._ |

**Do not use _wish_.** Use _want_ instead, or state the action directly.

| Avoid                                                 | Recommended                                           |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| _If you wish to change your notification settings..._ | _If you want to change your notification settings..._ |

**Avoid using _etc._ whenever possible.** Instead, use _such as_ or _including_. If you must use _etc._ be sure to place a period at the end, even it is followed by a comma.

| Avoid                                                                  | Recommended                                                                           |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _To create your account, enter your name, address, phone number, etc._ | _To create your account, enter information, such as name, address, and phone number._ |

**Avoid using _i.e._ whenever possible.** Instead, use _in other words_. If you must use _i.e._ be sure to place a period at the end.

| Avoid                                                                                                                             | Recommended                                                                                                                                 |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _To create your account, you will need to enter personally identifiable information, i.e., your name, address, and phone number._ | _To create your account, you will need to enter personally identifiable information, in other words, your name, address, and phone number._ |

**Avoid using _e.g._ whenever possible.** Instead, use _for example_. If you must use _e.g._ be sure to place a period at the end.

| Avoid                                                                                             | Recommended                                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _To create your account, enter your personal information, e.g., name, address, and phone number._ | _To create your account, enter your personal information, for example, your name, address, and phone number._ |

**Avoid using idioms or metaphors,** as they may not be widely understood.

| Avoid                                                                                   | Recommended                                                 |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| _You want your audience to be all ears and quiet as a mouse when reading your content._ | _You want your audience engaged when reading your content._ |

## Punctuation

**Use a serial or Oxford comma** when listing three or more items in a sentence. While the serial comma is not preferred in the ACME style guide, it is commonly used in technical writing to aid in reader understanding.

Example: _Susan was invited to a meeting with a new hire, her former manager and an HR professional._ In this example, Susan is meeting with a new hire who happens to be her former manager and works as an HR professional. When you add the serial comma, the meaning changes to indicate that Susan is meeting with three people: _Susan was invited to a meeting with a new hire, her former manager, and an HR professional._

**Be mindful of where periods are placed when ending your sentence with a parenthesis.** As a general rule, the period should fall inside the parentheses if the entire sentence is inside the parentheses. In contrast, the period should fall outside the parentheses if only a part of the sentence is inside the parentheses.

In a bulleted list, **whether you add a period to the items in a bulleted list** depends on if any of the items in the list are a complete sentence.

- If none of the items in a bulleted list is a complete sentence, you do not need to add a period at the end.
- If any one of the items in a bulleted list is a complete sentence, you should add a period to all the items in the list.

### Instructional Text

When stepping engineers through onscreen selections, such as checkboxes or data entry fields, **provide context as to why they are taking these particular steps** or what might happen if they deviate from those steps. This does not have to be an exhaustive explanation. However, providing insight into why selections are made, or not made, fosters a better understanding of the product.

When directing the reader to select screen elements, **bold the name of the screen element** to make it stand out from the rest of the content. Use bold on menu names, menu selections, dialog box names, fields names, buttons and so on. Consistent use of bolding promotes readability.

| Avoid                                                                                         | Recommended                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _To create a new document, from the File menu, select New. The New file dialog box displays._ | _To create a new document, from the **File** menu, select **New**. The **New File** dialog box displays._<br>OR<br>_To create a new document, select **File > New**. The **New File** dialog box displays._ |

## Processes

This section discusses best practices for documenting a process. Processes are documented with a sequence of numbered steps to accomplish a task.

Processes should start with an introductory sentence that provides context to the reader. However, the introductory sentence should not be a repeat of the section header. You can end the introductory sentence with a colon or a period. A colon should be used if the sentence precedes the process' steps. Use a complete sentence and not a partial sentence that is complete by the steps.

| Avoid                        | Recommended                                      |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| _To personalize the portal:_ | _To personalize the portal, follow these steps:_ |

If a procedure only has one step, do not number the step.

| Avoid                                          | Recommended                              |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| _To save your changes:_<br>1. Select **Save.** | _To save your changes, select **Save.**_ |

Each step in a procedure should have its own numbered step. However, you may consolidate small steps into one step when it makes sense to do so.

| Avoid                                                                 | Recommended                                                        |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| _To save your changes:_<br>1. Select **File.**<br>2. Select **Save.** | _To save your changes, select **File**, and then select **Save.**_ |

If your procedure contains **optional steps,** clearly indicate to the reader that the step is optional by adding the word _Optional_ followed by a colon (:) at the beginning of the step.

| Avoid                                                                                                                                     | Recommended                                                                                                                               |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1. To save your changes, select **File**, and then select **Save.**<br>2. Inform your teammates that the file has been updated (optional) | 1. To save your changes, select **File**, and then select **Save.**<br>2. Optional: Inform your teammates that the file has been updated. |

You should orient your reader by telling them what task they are completing before giving the direction of what to do. Orienting the reader also includes giving them guidance on where in the user interface the step occurs.

| Avoid                                                                                                               | Recommended                                                                                                               |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _Press **Ctrl+B** to bold the selected word._<br>OR<br>_Select **OK** to save your changes in the Edit dialog box._ | _To bold a word, select it and press **Ctrl+B.**_<br>OR<br>_In the Edit dialog box, to save your changes, select **OK.**_ |

## Notifications

All notifications should follow these best practices:

- **Use clear, succinct subject lines** â€” your subject line should quickly convey to the reader what the message is about and whether they need to act. Guidance on writing subject lines with easily understood prefixes for each notification type follows.
- **Keep them brief** â€” convey your message as concisely as possible, using bullet points when it makes sense to improve scanning.
- **Help the users find additional information** â€” include links if possible to any information supporting the message, such as new or updated product documentation.
- **Provide a means to ask questions** â€” include an email address or a Teams site where questions may be directed.
- **Follow the developer portal Content Style Guide** â€” follow the content writing guidelines to ensure consistency in the content.

## Release Notes

Release notes are a summary of what has changed with your product since the last release. They are important when highlighting a change that will directly affect your user's experience with your product. Whether it is a new feature within your product, a fix to something that was previously broken, or a change to the way it looks, release notes act as a guide for your user.

The following are the benefits of release notes:

- **They show progress:** Frequent and consistent product updates give users confidence that your product is evolving and delivering to its roadmap.
- **They foster engagement:** Release notes are a great way to engage your users at regular intervals by showing them what is new in your product.
- **They educate users:** You want your users to get as much value out of your product as they can, and release notes can be a great way to educate them on how best to use any new updates.
- **They set user expectations:** By informing and educating users on why a change in the product is better for them, you can head off some change resistance.

You should write a release note for the following:

- **New features:** It is important to update your users on what is new and how it benefits the user.
- **Bug fixes:** If there was a specific problem in your product and you corrected it, be sure to let your users know.
- **Product improvements:** Inform your users of enhancements to the product. Users want to know that the product is evolving.
- **Product deprecations:** Inform your users when features are removed. This shows that the team is mindful of removing unused or outdated features.

**Release note subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in product release notes.

_[Product Name] [announces or another verb] [feature / event summary]_

Examples:

- _Developer portal announces the release of in-context help_
- _Estate Management releases Security Toolkit 11_

## Announcements

An announcement may be used to provide product or product line information that falls outside of a release note. This could include information on product feature launch dates, upcoming events, or conferences where your product will be highlighted, updates to product documentation, or information regarding languages that are becoming end of life or platforms that are being retired.

When writing the announcement, be mindful of your objective. What is that you want the reader to know or do after reading the announcement? If due dates are involved, be sure that they clearly called out and that they are accurate.

**Announcement subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in product announcements.

_[Product Name] [announces or another verb] [event summary]_

Examples:

- _Developer portal invites you to attend an onboarding session_
- _OpenRewrite announces upcoming launch of 40 recipes_

## Incident Notifications

Incident notifications inform users of an issue with a product that is impacting users, but is not causing a service interruption.

Incident notifications should have a minimum of three messages associated with them.

- **Incident notice** â€” message issued as soon as possible after the issue has been detected.
- **Incident update** â€” message issued to communicate that the issue is ongoing and provide an overview of the resolution and an estimated resolution time if possible.
- **Incident resolved** â€” message issued when the issue is resolved and service is returned to normal.

**Incident subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in service issue notifications.

_Incident notice with [Product Name] [optional brief descriptor of issue]_
_Incident update with [Product Name] [optional brief descriptor of issue]_
_Incident resolved with [Product Name] [optional brief descriptor of issue]_

Examples:

- _**Incident notice**_ _with GAIA Portal experiencing latency_
- _**Incident update**_ _with GAIA Portal experiencing latency_
- _**Incident resolved**_ _with GAIA Portal experiencing latency_

## Planned Outage Notifications

Planned outage notifications inform users of the timing and duration of a planned service interruption. Any actions that the user should take prior to the outage window or after the outage window should also be conveyed.

Planned outage notifications should have a minimum of three messages associated with them: advanced notice, outage initiated, and service restored.

- **Advanced notice** â€” message issued far enough ahead of the planned outage window to allow users to plan for the service interruption. Two weeks in advance is a suggested minimum for this type of notification.
- **Outage initiated** â€” message issued when the planned outage window has started.
- **Service restored** â€” message issued when the planned outage window has closed and service has successfully been restored.
- **Outage issue** â€” message issued when issues are encountered during the planned outage which result in a delay to service restoration.

**Planned outage subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in planned outage notifications.

_Outage scheduled for [Product Name] on [Date]_
_Planned outage initiated for [Product Name]_
_Service restored for [Product Name] â€“ Planned Outage Complete_
_Delay in return to service for [Product Name] â€“ Planned Outage Ongoing_

Examples:

- _**Outage scheduled**_ _for JET Confluence on December 1, 2024_
- _**Planned outage initiated**_ _for JET Confluence_
- _**Service restored**_ _for JET Confluence â€“ Planned Outage Complete_
- _**Delay in return to service**_ _for JET Confluence â€” Planned Outage Ongoing_

## Unplanned Outage Notifications

Unplanned outage notifications create awareness of an unplanned service interruption and its impact to users. The notification should clearly communicate status and actions being taken to resolve.

Unplanned outage notifications should have a minimum of two messages associated with them â€“ notice and restoration. Update messages may be sent as needed depending on the length of the outage and the user impact.

- **Notice of service interruption** â€” message issued as soon as possible after the outage has been detected.
- **Service interruption update** â€” message issued to communicate that the issue is ongoing and provide an overview of the resolution and an estimated return to service time if possible.
- **Service restored** â€” message issued when the service has been restored.

**Unplanned outage subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in unplanned outage notifications.

_Service outage for [Product Name] on [Date]_
_Service outage update for [Product Name]_
_Service restored for [Product Name] â€“ Planned Outage Complete_

Examples:

- _**Service outage**_ _for the developer portal on December 1, 2024_
- _**Service outage update**_ _for JET Sonar_
- _**Service restored**_ _for JET Confluence_

## User Action Required

User action notifications communicate the need for your product's users to take an action and the date by which it should be taken.

User action notifications may be used for a variety of reasons but the underlying use is a call to action with a due date.

You should ensure that the content of the user action notification clearly communicates:

- The action to be taken
- Directions for completing the action
- The due date for the action
- Where users can access help if needed.

User action notifications should have a minimum of two messages â€“ notice of the action and a reminder to complete the action.

- **Notice of action** â€” message to create awareness of the action required, its impact to you, its due date, and a means for you to ask questions or learn more.
- **Reminder to complete** â€” message to remind users of action required and due date.

**User action required subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in user action notifications.

_Action Required for [Product Name] users by [Date]_
_Reminder: Action Required for [Product Name] users by [Date]_

Examples:

- _**Action Required**_ _for Developer Portal users by December 15, 2024_
- _**Reminder: Action Required**_ _for Developer Portal users by December 15, 2024_

## Process Notifications

Process notifications are issued one time to inform the user of what their actions have triggered â€“ initiation, completion, or failure.

The suggested statuses to be used throughout the developer portal, whenever possible, may be found in the Statuses section.

**Process subject line guidance:**

Follow this structure to help provide consistency in your process notifications.

**Status:** _[context] [Task/Process Name] [additional information as needed]_

Examples:

- **Submitted:** _Jane Smith user account creation process initiated_
- **Success:** _Jane Smith user account creation approved_
- **In Progress:** _Jane Smith user account being provisioned_
- **Failed:** _Jane Smith user account creation encountered an error_
